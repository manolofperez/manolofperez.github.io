Welcome to Manolo Perez's website. My main research interests lie in the diversification of Neotropical taxa. Using climatic, spatial, computational, and genetic tools, my aim is to study demographic events and phylogenetic relationships in different taxa. I am currently studying the diversification of fish species from the Osteoglossiformes order that occur on three different continents, using phylogenetic, biogeographic, and machine learning methods. During my PhD, my studies focused on the phylogeography of species associated with dry environments. I have also worked with diversification mechanisms in other plant taxa, birds, and arthropods in the Neotropics.

Main Research Lines

Phylogeography

Comparative phylogeography models using different sources of data and statistical methods.

Species Distribution Modeling

Modeling species distribution from environmental data.

Landscape Features from Remote Sensing Data

Using remote sensing data to gain insights on landscape features and evaluate species distribution considering key ecological attributes.

Main Skills

Bioinformatics

Analysis of NGS datasets, assessment of genetic diversity, phylogenetic relationships, and demographic history.

Remote Sensing for Species Distribution

Using remote sensing and environmental data to assess potential species distribution in past, present, and future conditions.

Develop Markers in Non-Model Organisms

Development of microsatellite and SNP/sequence datasets in non-model organisms.

Machine Learning

Training models to handle genetic and phenotypic data.
