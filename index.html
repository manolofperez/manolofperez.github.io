Welcome to Manolo Perez website. My main research interests lie in the diversification of Neotropical taxa. Using climatic, spatial, computational and genetic tools, my aim is to study demographic events and phylogenetic relationships on different taxa. I am currently studying the diversification of fish species from the Osteoglossiformes order that occur in three different continents, using phylogenetic, biogeographic and machine learning methods. During my PhD my studies focused on phylogeography of species associated to dry environments. I also worked with diversification mechanisms on other plant taxa, birds and arthropods in the Neotropics.

Main Research Lines

Phylogeography

Compare concurrent phylogeography models using different sources of data and statistical methods.

Species Distribution Modeling

Modeling species distribution from environmental data.

Landscape Features from Remote Sensing Data

Use of Remote Sensing data to gain insights on landscape features and evaluate species distribution considering key ecological attributes.

Main Skills

Bioinformatics

Analysis to treat NGS dataset, assess genetic diversity, phylogenetic relationships and demographic history.

Remote Sensing for Species Distribution

Use of remote sensing and environmental data to asses potential species distribution in past, present and future conditions.

Develop markers in non-model organisms

Development of microsatellite and SNP/sequence datasets in non-model organisms.

Machine Learning

Train models to handle genetic and phenotipic data.
